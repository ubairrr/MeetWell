**Section 1: Executive Summary**
CRITICAL: This section must contain zero technical terms. No mention of Electron, SQLCipher, Deepgram, React, WebSocket, npm, Zod, IPC, or any library names. Write for a sophisticated investor who does not know software.

Cover these elements (sourced from RSCH-01 and PROJECT.md):

1.1 What is MeetingAssist (2–3 sentences): A macOS desktop assistant that sits on the side of your screen during any meeting — in-person, video call, or phone — and automatically produces a complete record: minutes, key decisions, action items with deadlines, and a full summary. You walk out of every meeting with a document you can share and deadlines you can schedule — without having taken a single note. The assistant is invisible to meeting participants and works locally on your device; nothing you say is stored on a cloud server you don't control.

1.2 The Problem: Knowledge workers spend 12–15 hours per week in meetings. 65% of meetings are considered unproductive (RSCH-01). The outcomes that matter — decisions made, tasks assigned, follow-up dates agreed — are captured only by whoever took notes, which means they are partial, biased, and often wrong. Post-meeting tools that use AI "bots" require participants to accept an uninvited bot, raise recording objections, and send all audio to the tool vendor's servers. These bots are increasingly banned by enterprise IT. MeetingAssist captures from your own device, with your own disclosure, with your control over the data.

1.3 Market Signal: The AI meeting assistant market is $3.67B and growing at 34.7% CAGR. Granola (the closest single-product analog) raised $43M in early 2025 on a similar premise, validating the space. The primary customer is knowledge workers at high-meeting-volume teams: consulting, investment, legal, product, and engineering. (Source: RSCH-01)

1.4 Five Differentiating Pillars (from RSCH-01, non-technical framing):
1. No visible bot: Works through your own computer's microphone and speakers — no bot account joins your meeting, no uninvited participant appears, no IT policy triggered
2. You own your data: Everything is stored on your device, encrypted. You can delete a meeting record at any time. Nothing is uploaded to a cloud storage service.
3. Trustworthy records: Every action item and every decision in the generated minutes is traceable to a direct quote from the conversation. The system shows you the exact words that support each item.
4. Real-time awareness: A running summary of the last 5 minutes is always visible on the side of your screen. If you step away, you get a digest of what you missed the moment you return.
5. Private by design: The founding privacy commitment — disclosed recording only, full consent before capture starts, all-party disclosure default — is not a feature toggle; it is the product's core posture.

1.5 Monetization Hypothesis (from RSCH-01):
- Free trial first: A time-limited free tier (e.g., 3 meetings) removes the activation barrier and lets the product prove its value before asking for payment.
- Paid tier: $12–15/month per user (individual), with a team tier at ~$10/user/month above 5 seats. Pricing is benchmarked against Granola ($18/mo) and Otter.ai ($16.99/mo) — positioned below premium bots, justified by the no-bot and privacy advantages.
- Revenue model: Subscription SaaS (monthly/annual). No usage-based pricing in v1 — simplicity lowers activation friction.
- Success metric hypothesis: Free trial to paid conversion rate >= 25% (industry benchmark for B2C SaaS tools with a strong free-to-paid value unlock).

3.1 Consent & Recording Posture (DEC-01)
Summary: MeetingAssist is a disclosed, consent-first recorder. Per-meeting consent gate is a hard precondition to capture — the app does not start recording until the user has checked a consent disclosure checkbox. Disclosed-not-covert posture is unconditional. Hiding the user's own overlay panel from their own screen-share is acceptable (technical invisibility of the panel); concealing the fact of recording is never acceptable (DEC-01 §2).
Link: [02-DEC-01-consent-recording-posture.md](../02-foundational-decisions-adrs/02-DEC-01-consent-recording-posture.md)

3.2 Data Handling & Privacy (DEC-02)
Summary: Local-first storage with SQLCipher AES-256 full-database encryption and macOS Keychain-backed key. Raw audio is deleted after transcription. Transcripts are retained indefinitely until the user deletes them. No cloud storage. On-device offline mode (whisper.cpp) is designed in as an optional path for users who require zero cloud contact. All API keys are stored in the macOS Keychain — never in plaintext config files.
Link: [02-DEC-02-data-handling-privacy.md](../02-foundational-decisions-adrs/02-DEC-02-data-handling-privacy.md)

3.3 AI Faithfulness Contract (04-AI-SPEC)
Summary: Every extracted artifact — every action item, every decision, every date — is generated through a mandatory two-stage protocol: Stage 1 extracts verbatim quotes from the transcript; Stage 2 generates artifact content ONLY from those quotes. All artifacts are proposals pending user confirmation. Users can toggle a "Verify" panel to see the exact transcript quote supporting any item. No artifact is ever auto-written to any external system (calendar, task manager).
Link: [04-AI-SPEC.md](../04-ai-grounding-context-spec-ai-spec/04-AI-SPEC.md)

3.4 Technology Stack Decisions (from CLAUDE.md)
A table: "Decision Area" | "Choice" | "Rationale"
- Capture library: audiotee (Core Audio Taps) primary; Chromium loopback fallback — RSCH-04 spike validated; pre-mixer audio, no persistent purple indicator
- STT provider: Deepgram Nova-3 with diarization — 61.5% meeting-domain accuracy improvement over Nova-2; free diarization up to 12 speakers
