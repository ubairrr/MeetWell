  <action>
    Create .planning/phases/03-deep-research/03-RSCH-01-REPORT.md with the following required sections:

    **## Primary Persona**: Define primary persona as independent knowledge workers and small teams (PMs, founders, consultants, managers with back-to-back meetings, privacy-conscious, blocked by cloud-only tools). Secondary persona: remote/hybrid teams needing consistent meeting artifacts across Zoom, Meet, Teams, and in-person.

    **## Competitive Landscape**: Include the 6-tool comparison table (Granola, Fathom, Fireflies, Otter.ai, Notion AI, Meetily) with columns: Approach, Pricing (Business tier), Privacy Posture, Key Strength, Weakness. Key data points: Granola $14/user/mo + $43M raise May 2025 + audio never uploaded; Fathom $15/user/mo + unlimited free tier + bot-optional; Fireflies $19/user/mo + BIPA lawsuit Dec 2025; Otter ~$20/user/mo + class-action Aug 2025; Notion AI $20/user/mo (Business required); Meetily free + 100% local.

    **## Positioning**: MeetingAssist's differentiation story: local-first encrypted storage + no-bot + dual-channel capture + live in-meeting intelligence + production-grade artifact extraction. Note the positioning gap vs Granola (cloud transcripts, no cross-meeting memory, no encrypted local storage). Cite market data: 46–50% of workers cite privacy/security as top reason to avoid these tools; 84% alter speech when AI is visibly present.

    **## Monetization Model**: Recommended hypothesis — free trial (14–30 days, full features, no minute cap) → Pro at $12–15/user/month, unlimited meetings, full artifact suite; no business tier for v1 (single-user product). Rationale: trials/reverse trials convert better than permanent free tiers in 2025–2026; usage caps are primary user frustration point; Granola's 30-day history cap is effective "aha moment" trigger.

    **## PROJECT.md TBD Resolutions**: Explicitly resolve each TBD:
    - Customer: Independent knowledge workers and small teams (PMs, founders, consultants, managers); privacy-conscious; back-to-back meeting workflows; macOS users.
    - Revenue Model: Subscription (monthly/annual). Free trial first (14–30 days). Pro tier $12–15/user/month. No minute caps. Annual discount ~40%.
    - Success Metric (hypothesis for PRD): Trial-to-Pro conversion rate as primary. MRR growth as secondary. These require user research validation — the PRD should recommend a measurement plan.

    Cite all data from 03-RESEARCH.md, noting [CITED] vs [ASSUMED] markers from the source.
